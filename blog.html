Photography and Me: How it Began

My hands felt the grip of a DSLR summer 2015, right before college. I was visiting China and was traveling with my uncle, when he decided to give me his Canon Kiss x4 so that I could capture some memories on my trip. He taught me the basics, and the rest has been a self-taught journey of online guides and self-experimenting. My goal in the beginning was to just shoot as much as possible. Whenever I had time, I was out exploring and working on my skills.

New Knowledge: New Styles

Freshman year, I became the Historian for the Chinese American Society, and that's where I really gained my foundation for shooting portraits, groups, and indoor shots. I also started playing around with night photography, and developed a big passion for long exposure photography. Looking at the Instagrams of Professional Photographers really changed the way I looked at composition and heightened my sense of creativity.

Two Years Later: Where am I Now?

Recently coming back from a summer trip to China and Japan, I was able to witness some scenic views and capture some amazing shots. I've been shooting Senior Grad Pics, Ring Dance Photoshoots, and spontaneous shoots for friends here and there, and plan on continuing that. I'll never get tired of what I do; Adjusting hues/saturations to make an image pop to life, exploring new cities and capturing iconic views, and seeing friends put up new pictures from a recent shoot with me. These are sensations that not many others could feel, and ones that makes me proud to keep pressing that shutter.
